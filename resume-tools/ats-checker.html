How ATS Keyword Checking Works

Our ATS keyword checker analyzes your resume text using advanced algorithms that evaluate multiple factors affecting how Applicant Tracking Systems parse and score your resume. The tool examines your resume content comprehensively to identify strengths and areas for improvement.

The checker evaluates your resume for:

  • Keyword Density: Frequency of important industry keywords and their distribution throughout your resume. Optimal keyword density helps ATS systems identify relevant candidates.
  • ATS-Friendly Terms: Common terms and phrases that ATS systems recognize and prioritize. These include technical skills, certifications, and industry-standard terminology.
  • Formatting Issues: Problems that might cause ATS parsing errors, such as complex layouts, tables, graphics, or non-standard formatting that prevents proper text extraction.
  • Content Structure: Organization and readability for ATS systems. Well-structured resumes with clear sections are easier for ATS systems to parse and categorize.
  • Keyword Placement: Strategic placement of keywords in headers, job titles, and throughout the resume content for maximum ATS visibility.

How the ATS Score is Calculated

The ATS compatibility score is calculated based on multiple weighted factors that reflect how ATS systems typically evaluate resumes. The scoring algorithm considers:

  • Keyword Relevance (30%): Presence of common industry keywords, technical skills, and job-relevant terminology
  • Content Quality (25%): Use of action verbs, quantifiable achievements, and professional language
  • Structure and Formatting (20%): Clear section headers, consistent formatting, and ATS-friendly layout
  • Keyword Density (15%): Appropriate frequency and distribution of keywords throughout the resume
  • ATS Compatibility Indicators (10%): Presence of standard sections, proper date formats, and absence of problematic elements

Important Note: This score is an estimate based on common ATS evaluation patterns. Actual ATS systems use proprietary algorithms that vary by vendor (Taleo, Workday, Greenhouse, etc.) and employer. The score should be used as guidance to improve your resume, not as a guarantee of ATS compatibility. Factors like job description matching, industry-specific requirements, and employer-specific preferences also significantly impact ATS screening results.

What Makes a Resume ATS-Friendly?

ATS systems are designed to parse and categorize resume content automatically. To maximize compatibility, your resume should:

  • Include Relevant Keywords: Incorporate keywords from the job description naturally throughout your resume, especially in job titles, skills sections, and experience descriptions
  • Use Simple Formatting: Avoid complex layouts, tables, graphics, and multi-column designs that confuse ATS parsing engines
  • Employ Standard Sections: Use clear, standard section headers like "Experience", "Education", "Skills", "Certifications" that ATS systems easily recognize
  • Place Keywords Strategically: Include keywords in multiple locations - headers, job titles, skills lists, and experience descriptions - for maximum visibility
  • Choose Appropriate File Format: Use .docx or .pdf format. Avoid .txt for formatted resumes, and never use image-based PDFs
  • Use Standard Fonts: Stick to common fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman) that ATS systems can reliably parse
  • Avoid Special Characters: Minimize use of symbols, graphics, or special characters that may not parse correctly

This tool is essential for job seekers at all career stages. Recent graduates can ensure their resumes are optimized for automated screening systems. Common ATS Keywords by Industry

Different industries value different keywords, and our checker looks for common terms across various fields. Understanding industry-specific keywords helps you optimize your resume effectively:

  • Technical Skills: Programming languages (Python, Java, JavaScript), software tools (Salesforce, Tableau), frameworks, and technical certifications
  • Soft Skills: Leadership, communication, teamwork, problem-solving, project management, and collaboration
  • Industry-Specific Terms: Professional terminology, methodologies, and standards relevant to your field
  • Action Verbs: Managed, developed, implemented, created, improved, led, optimized - these demonstrate impact and achievement
  • Quantifiable Achievements: Percentages, numbers, metrics, and measurable results that demonstrate value and impact
  • Certifications and Credentials: Professional certifications, licenses, and credentials that are important in your industry

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good ATS score?
A score above 70% is considered good, while 80%+ is excellent. However, the score is just one indicator. Focus on incorporating relevant keywords from the job description.
Should I include every keyword from the job description?
Not necessarily. Focus on keywords that are relevant to your experience and skills. Keyword stuffing can actually hurt your resume's readability.
How often should I check my resume?
Check your resume each time you apply for a new position, as different jobs require different keywords. Tailor your resume for each application.
Is my resume data stored?
No, all analysis happens in your browser. Your resume never leaves your device, ensuring complete privacy.
